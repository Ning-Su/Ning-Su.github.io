Wildfire News in New York State

Wildfire is defined as an uncontrolled fire spreading through natural or unnatural vegetation that often has the potential to threaten lives and property if not contained. Wildfire in New York is based on the same science and environmental factors as any wildfire in the world. Fuels, weather and topography are the primary factors that determine the natural spread and destruction of every wildfire. According to Forest Ranger Division wildfire occurrence data from 1993 through 2017, 95% of wildfires in New York are caused by humans while lightning is responsible for 5%. Debris burning accounts for 33% of all wildfires, incendiary fires account for 16%, campfires cause 16% and children are responsible for 4%. Smoking, equipment, railroads and 6miscellaneous causes contribute to the remaining 25% of wildfires.

Source from: https://www.dec.ny.gov/lands/42378.html

NYS Forest Ranger Wildland Fire Reporting

The dataset includes details for each wildland fire recorded between 1975 and 2007 under the jurisdiction of the DEC NYS Forest Rangers. The slider on the right top of the map allows people choose the start date of different wildfires each year. The size of the circle on the map show the acreage of each wildfire, and the legend with different colors below the slider shows the different causes of wildfire. When people hover the mouse on each circle, it will shows detailed information about the wildfire represented by this circle, including the acreage, incident name, county name, and fuel model.

Data Source: https://www.kaggle.com/new-york-state/nys-forest-ranger-wildland-fire-reporting
